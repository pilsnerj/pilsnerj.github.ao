Making my mold with 3D builder

Building a mold in 3D builder was actually quite easy . Within the insert section it all has all the basic necessary edits that I had needed to be able to get measurements, Boolean difference, in ways for me to build out the wall . I was also able to experiment, and I learned that I could also do splits so it would be a great program to do a two part mold in as well so I could do a two part candle mold within paint 3D as well. I decided to make a heart sheet mold, and I decided that it made sense to do just one mold rather than a full tray because that would take up both more time to print an more materials which went planning for an entire class project definitely want to limit that . So I decided to make a heart shape either ice maker mold or soap mold and for my own product I decided to make soup because I didn't have food safe oomoo. I was really excited when it exported a great mesh and I got it printing. I was able to make my silicone mold successfully and pull it out. I then bought the base for soap, added some essential oils, and dried Flowers to my mold. I was able to successfully finish my soap models, and have concluded that this would be a great activity even down to the 4th grade level. 3D builder was quite easy to use all you needed to do was create a box then take the difference with another box Ann you had the walls of the tray then I was able to create my heart model inside get that leveled right and then I was able to remove any pieces that were hanging off the edge with some of the split components. I think there could be a lot of flexibility in what students create in simple ways and we got them to think about what are they going to be able to pull out and just work through the workflow of making a mold for mold and what that should look like. I'm really excited to do it with kids. I also love my soap!
